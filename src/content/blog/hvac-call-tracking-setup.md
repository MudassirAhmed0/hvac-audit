"Where did you hear about us?"

That's not a marketing strategy. That's a guess wrapped in a question most customers can't accurately answer.

If you can't tell whether your leads come from Google Ads, organic search, your [Google Business Profile](/blog/hvac-google-business-profile/), or a yard sign, you're making every marketing decision blind. You might be spending [$5,000/month on a channel](/blog/hvac-marketing-budget/) that produces zero revenue.

Here's how to fix that for $0.

## The free Google setup

Google provides free call tracking through Google Ads and Google Business Profile. If you're running Google Ads (even at a small budget), you already have access.

### Google Ads call tracking

1. In your Google Ads account, go to Settings > Conversions
2. Add a new conversion action > Phone calls
3. Select "Calls from ads" and "Calls from your website"
4. Install the Google forwarding number snippet on your site

Google replaces your phone number on the website with a tracking number (only for visitors who came from ads). When that number rings, Google records it as a conversion.

Now you know: this call came from Google Ads. This one didn't.

### Google Business Profile call tracking

If someone taps "Call" on your GBP listing, Google already tracks this in your GBP Insights dashboard. Check it monthly: "Calls from your Business Profile" shows how many people called directly from Maps or Search.

This is free. It's already running. Most HVAC contractors have never looked at it.

## The $30/month upgrade (CallRail or similar)

For contractors who want to track every channel, call tracking services like CallRail or CallTrackingMetrics offer dedicated tracking numbers for each marketing source.

How it works:
- **Number 1** on your website (for organic visitors)
- **Number 2** on your Google Ads landing pages
- **Number 3** on your GBP listing
- **Number 4** on your truck wraps / yard signs
- **Number 5** on your direct mail pieces

All numbers forward to your main office line. The customer notices nothing different. But you see exactly which channel each call came from.

At $30-$50/month for 5 tracking numbers, this is the highest-ROI marketing expense you'll ever make.

## What to track (and what it tells you)

### Calls per channel per month

| Channel | Calls | Booked Jobs | Revenue | Cost | ROI |
|---------|-------|-------------|---------|------|-----|
| Google Ads | 45 | 12 | $18,000 | $3,000 | 6x |
| Organic | 30 | 10 | $15,000 | $1,500 | 10x |
| GBP | 25 | 8 | $12,000 | $0 | — |
| HomeAdvisor | 20 | 3 | $4,500 | $2,000 | 2.25x |

Without tracking, these numbers are invisible. With tracking, the decision is obvious: organic and GBP are your best channels. [HomeAdvisor is your worst](/blog/best-lead-source-hvac/). Now you know where to invest more and what to cut.

### Call duration

Short calls (under 60 seconds) are usually not real leads — wrong numbers, existing customers checking hours, spam. Calls over 2 minutes are almost always real prospects.

Filter your conversion data by call duration to get accurate lead counts. Most contractors overcount leads because they count every call, including the 20-second ones.

### Peak call times

When do most of your calls come in? If 40% of calls hit between 5pm and 9pm but your office closes at 5pm, you need an [after-hours solution](/blog/hvac-website-not-getting-calls/) or you're losing 40% of your leads.

## Form tracking (the other half)

Phone calls aren't your only conversions. Form submissions matter too — especially on mobile where some users prefer to submit a request rather than call.

Track form submissions by adding Google Analytics event tracking to every form on your site. Or use your CRM to tag the source of each form submission.

The combination of call tracking + form tracking gives you a complete picture of your lead flow. Without it, the [$4,200/month you're losing](/blog/hvac-website-costing-4200-per-month/) stays invisible.

## The decision it enables

Once you know your numbers, every marketing decision becomes simple math:

- Channel A costs $200/customer → **keep and scale**
- Channel B costs $500/customer → **optimize or cut**
- Channel C costs $50/customer → **invest heavily**

No opinions. No gut feelings. No "I think the radio ads are working." Just data.

Set up Google's free call tracking this afternoon. It takes 30 minutes. Next month, you'll have data. The month after, you'll make your first data-driven marketing decision. And you'll wonder how you ever ran a business without it.
