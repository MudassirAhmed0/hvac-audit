You can get a website for $500. Plenty of freelancers and template shops will set one up in a week. And for a lot of HVAC contractors, that sounds reasonable. You're in the business of fixing air conditioners, not building websites.

But here's the thing about a $500 website: it costs you way more than $500.

## What $500 actually buys you

A $500 website is typically: a WordPress template with your logo slapped on, stock photos, a generic "Services" page, your phone number somewhere, and shared hosting that costs $5/month.

It looks like a website. It technically is a website. But it doesn't do the one thing a website is supposed to do — generate leads.

## The hidden costs

### Slow loading = lost visitors

Cheap hosting and unoptimized templates mean [load times of 10-18 seconds](/blog/18-seconds-your-customer-left-after-3/). Over [53% of visitors leave](/blog/53-percent-leave-before-site-loads/) after 3 seconds. Your $500 site is invisible to more than half the people who click on it.

### No mobile optimization = lost mobile users

64-76% of HVAC searches happen on mobile. A $500 template that looks passable on desktop but breaks on a phone screen is losing the majority of your traffic. Buttons too small to tap. Text you have to pinch to read. Forms that don't work on iOS.

### No SSL = Chrome warnings

If the developer didn't set up HTTPS (many don't), Chrome shows "Not Secure" in the URL bar. 82% of sites in our [147-site audit](/blog/hvac-website-audit-147-sites/) had this problem. Would you call a business your browser is actively warning you about?

### No SEO = no organic traffic

A $500 site has no keyword research, no meta descriptions, no schema markup, no individual service pages. It won't rank for anything. Which means the only traffic you'll ever get is from paid ads — and those clicks land on a site that doesn't convert them.

### No conversion optimization = dead ends

No click-to-call button. No after-hours form. No chat. No financing page. One generic contact form buried under a navigation menu. Every [lead killer we've documented](/blog/hvac-website-not-getting-calls/) shows up on a $500 site.

## The math

The average HVAC website we audit [costs its owner $4,200 per month](/blog/hvac-website-costing-4200-per-month/) in missed leads. Let's be conservative and say a $500 site costs you just $2,000/month in lost leads.

| | $500 site | $5,000 site |
|---|----------|------------|
| Upfront cost | $500 | $5,000 |
| Monthly lost leads | ~$2,000 | ~$200 |
| Year 1 total cost | $24,500 | $7,400 |
| Year 2 total cost | $48,500 | $9,800 |

The $500 site costs **3x more** in the first year and **5x more** by year two. And these are conservative numbers.

## What a proper HVAC website costs

A website that actually generates leads for an HVAC contractor costs $3,000-$8,000 to build and $50-$150/month to maintain. That includes:

- Fast, reliable hosting
- Mobile-first responsive design
- SSL certificate
- Individual service pages with SEO
- Click-to-call on every page
- Contact forms with after-hours messaging
- Review integration
- Google Business Profile alignment
- Schema markup for local search
- Core Web Vitals optimization

The ROI is immediate. Even converting 2-3 extra leads per month at $300-$500 average ticket covers the entire investment.

## The real question

It's not "can I afford a $5,000 website?" It's "can I afford to lose $2,000+ every month because I saved $4,500 upfront?"

Look at the difference between sites like [Air Tech of Houston](/report/air-tech-of-houston-ac-and-plumbing/) (score: 30) and [Rescue Air and Plumbing](/report/rescue-air-and-plumbing/) (score: 80). One is leaking leads everywhere. One is capturing them. The investment gap between their websites is probably $3,000-$5,000. The revenue gap is tens of thousands per year.
