Google's AI Overviews now appear in more than half of all search results. When someone Googles "how much does AC repair cost," they get an AI-generated summary at the top — often without clicking a single website.

ChatGPT and other AI assistants are answering questions like "who's the best HVAC contractor near me" using data from review sites, Google Business Profiles, and structured website content.

This isn't coming. It's here. And most HVAC contractors are invisible to it.

## What changed

### Google AI Overviews

Previously, Google showed 10 blue links. Now, for many searches, it shows an AI-generated answer first. The answer cites sources — and if your website isn't structured in a way AI can read, you're not getting cited.

For HVAC contractors, this matters most for informational searches: "AC making noise," "how often to change air filter," "HVAC repair vs replace." These are top-of-funnel searches where homeowners start their research.

### Voice search

"Hey Google, find me an AC repair company near me" is increasingly how people search. Voice search favors businesses with strong [Google Business Profiles](/blog/hvac-google-business-profile/), high review counts, and structured data that Google can read clearly.

### AI assistants (ChatGPT, etc.)

When someone asks ChatGPT "who's the best HVAC company in Dallas," it pulls from review data, business directories, and website content. The companies that show up are the ones with the strongest, most consistent online presence — not necessarily the biggest or best at HVAC.

## What HVAC contractors need to do

### 1. Add FAQ schema to your website

FAQ schema is structured data that tells Google (and AI) exactly what questions your page answers. It looks like this in your site's code:

```
Q: How much does AC repair cost in Dallas?
A: AC repair in Dallas typically costs $150-$500 depending on...
```

When you mark up your content with FAQ schema, Google can pull your answers directly into AI Overviews. Without it, your content is just text that AI may or may not find.

Add FAQ sections to your service pages, your homepage, and your blog posts. Mark them up with schema.

### 2. Write content that answers specific questions

AI pulls from content that directly answers questions. Not content that talks around a topic — content that clearly states the answer.

Bad: "Our experienced technicians provide top-quality AC repair services for homeowners in the greater Dallas area."

Good: "AC repair in Dallas costs between $150 and $500 for most common issues. Emergency after-hours repairs typically run $300-$800. We provide free diagnostics with any repair."

The second version is what AI wants to cite. It has specific numbers, direct answers, and useful information.

### 3. Strengthen your Google Business Profile

AI assistants heavily weight GBP data. [Weekly posts, active review management](/blog/hvac-google-business-profile/), complete service listings, and Q&A sections all feed AI more data about your business.

The contractors who maintain active GBPs are the ones AI recommends. The ones with dormant profiles don't exist in AI search.

### 4. Build topical authority

AI favors websites that cover a topic comprehensively. If you have one page about "AC repair" and nothing else, AI sees you as a thin resource. If you have 15 pages covering specific repair types, costs, FAQs, and maintenance guides, AI sees you as an authority.

This is where consistent blogging pays off. Each post adds to your site's topical depth. Over time, AI starts citing you more frequently because you've demonstrated expertise.

### 5. Get your data consistent everywhere

AI aggregates data from multiple sources: your website, GBP, Yelp, BBB, industry directories. If your business name, address, phone number, or service list differs across these platforms, AI gets confused and may show inaccurate information — or skip you entirely.

Audit your listings. Make them identical everywhere.

## What doesn't change

The fundamentals still matter more than AI optimization. [Site speed](/blog/hvac-website-speed-emergency-calls/), [mobile usability](/blog/hvac-website-not-getting-calls/), [review volume](/blog/hvac-google-reviews-strategy/), and conversion optimization are still the foundation. AI search builds on these signals — it doesn't replace them.

A contractor with a fast website, 500 Google reviews, and comprehensive content will dominate both traditional and AI search. A contractor with none of these won't be found anywhere.

## The opportunity

Most HVAC contractors haven't adapted to AI search yet. The ones who add schema markup, write answer-focused content, and maintain active online profiles now will have a significant advantage over the next 1-2 years.

This is an early-mover opportunity. The playbook is clear. The question is who executes first.
