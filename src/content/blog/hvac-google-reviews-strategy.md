You have hundreds of happy customers. Your Google profile has 47 reviews. Your competitor down the street — who you know does worse work — has 380.

Guess who shows up in the Map Pack.

Reviews are the single most visible trust signal in local search. They directly impact your [Google Business Profile ranking](/blog/hvac-google-business-profile/), your click-through rate, and whether someone calls you or scrolls past.

The problem isn't that your customers are unhappy. It's that only 5-10% of satisfied customers leave a review unprompted. You need a system.

## The timing window

The best time to ask for a review is **within 2 hours of completing the job.** The customer is still feeling the relief of having their problem fixed. The experience is fresh. Their phone is in their hand.

After 24 hours, the conversion rate on review requests drops by half. After a week, it's nearly zero. The job is forgotten. The goodwill faded.

## The ask that works

### In person (highest conversion)

Have your technician ask before leaving: "If you were happy with the service today, a Google review really helps us out. I can text you the link right now — takes about 30 seconds."

Key elements: it's personal, it's immediate, and you're removing friction by sending the link. Conversion rate: 30-40%.

### Text message (second best)

Send an automated text within 1 hour of job completion:

> "Hi [Name], thanks for choosing [Company] today! If you have 30 seconds, a Google review really helps our small business: [direct review link]. Thanks! — [Tech name]"

Keep it short. Include a direct link to your Google review page (not your website, not your Google listing — the direct "leave a review" URL). Conversion rate: 15-25%.

### Email (lowest but still works)

Same message, same timing. Email works better for larger jobs (installations) where the customer had more interaction with your team. Conversion rate: 5-10%.

## Getting the direct review link

1. Google your business name
2. Click your Google Business Profile
3. Click "Ask for reviews"
4. Copy the link Google provides

Or manually: `https://search.google.com/local/writereview?placeid=[YOUR_PLACE_ID]`

This link drops the customer directly into the review form. No searching, no clicking through your profile. One tap and they're writing.

## The system (automated)

Here's how to get 15+ reviews per month without thinking about it:

1. **Job completes** → technician asks in person
2. **1 hour later** → automated text with review link
3. **24 hours later** → follow-up email (only if no review yet)
4. **That's it.** Three touches max. Never more.

Most field service software (ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber) can automate steps 2 and 3. If yours can't, a simple CRM or even a Google Sheets + Zapier setup works.

With 80-100 jobs per month and a 15-20% review rate from this system, you'll hit 15+ reviews monthly. Consistently.

## What NOT to do

- **Don't offer incentives** — "Leave a review for 10% off" violates Google's terms and risks getting your reviews stripped
- **Don't ask for 5 stars** — ask for an "honest review." Google's algorithm can detect and discount suspicious review patterns
- **Don't ask unhappy customers** — screen first. If the job had issues, fix the issue before asking for a review
- **Don't batch-ask** — 30 reviews in one day after months of nothing looks fake to Google. Steady velocity matters more than spikes

## Responding to reviews

Respond to every review within 24 hours. Every. Single. One.

**5-star reviews:** Thank them by name, mention the specific service. "Thanks, Mike! Glad we could get your AC running before the weekend. Appreciate the trust."

**1-3 star reviews:** Acknowledge the concern, take responsibility where appropriate, move it offline. "Sorry to hear about your experience, Sarah. That's not our standard. I'd like to make this right — please call me directly at [number]."

Google has confirmed that response rate and quality factor into local rankings. And prospective customers read your responses to negative reviews more carefully than the reviews themselves.

## The compound effect

Review velocity — how many new reviews you get per month — matters more than total count. A business adding 15 reviews per month will outrank a competitor with a higher total but only 2-3 new reviews per month.

In markets like [Texas](/blog/hvac-seo-texas/), where the top Map Pack results have 300-800+ reviews, steady velocity is the only way to close the gap. If you're getting 15/month, you'll add 180 in a year. That changes your competitive position entirely.

Look at [The Chill Brothers](/report/the-chill-brothers/) with their massive review count and 70 audit score, versus [Veteran Air](/report/veteran-air/) at 45 with fewer reviews. Reviews are a direct predictor of online performance.
